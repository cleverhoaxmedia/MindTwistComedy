About the Event

Journey 3,000 Years Into the Past

Ages 6 – 14 · All Children Must Be Accompanied by an Adult

Step inside the world of ancient Egypt and discover the secrets buried beneath the sands of time. This immersive, two-day educational experience brings the world's greatest ancient civilization to vivid life for young curious minds.

Through hands-on activities, dramatic storytelling, and artifact replicas, children will explore the rise and fall of dynasties, the engineering marvel of the pyramids, the mysteries of the afterlife, and the everyday life of ancient Egyptians — from farmers and scribes to pharaohs and gods.

All sessions are led by accredited educators and guest scholars. Every ticket includes an activity booklet, hieroglyph name card, and a souvenir replica scarab amulet.

𓆣 Hands-On Artifact Station

Handle museum-quality replica artifacts — canopic jars, shabtis, amulets, and more. Children learn to think like an archaeologist.

𓂀 Hieroglyph Workshop

Write your name in ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs on authentic papyrus paper to take home as a keepsake.

𓅭 Gods & Myths Theater

A live 20-minute theatrical retelling of the myth of Osiris, Isis, and the origins of the afterlife, performed for each session.

★ Mummy Unwrapping Experience

Premium add-on: witness the live scientific unwrapping of a genuine mummy with expert commentary from a visiting professor. Limited availability.

Program

What to Expect

Each session runs approximately 2 hours (3 hours for Pharaoh's Circle). Schedule is identical across all three daily sessions.

0:00 – 0:20
Welcome & Introduction
Costumed guides greet guests and set the scene with a dramatic opening. Children receive their explorer kits and activity booklets.
0:20 – 0:45
Exhibition Hall Tour
Guided walk through the Life Along the Nile, Age of the Pharaohs, and Pyramid Builders galleries. Artifact handling stations open throughout.
0:45 – 1:05
Gods & Myths Theater
Live dramatic performance of the myth of Osiris and Isis. Interactive — audience members are invited to play small roles!
1:05 – 1:40
Hieroglyph Workshop
Guided workshop using reed-style brushes and ink on real papyrus. Children learn to write their names in hieroglyphs and take the papyrus home.
1:40 – 2:00
Wrap-Up, Certificates & Photo Time
Junior Archaeologist certificates awarded. Photo opportunity at the full-scale sarcophagus replica. Gift shop opens.
2:00 – 3:00 · PHARAOH'S CIRCLE ONLY ★
The Mummy Unwrapping Experience
Dr. Amira Saleh leads an intimate scientific demonstration of an authentic mummy unwrapping in the Unwrapping Chamber. Includes a guided lecture on mummification, ancient embalming practices, and the role of the afterlife in Egyptian culture. A live Q&A follows.
Our Guest Scholar

Meet Dr. Amira Saleh

𓅓

Dr. Amira Saleh, Ph.D.

Professor of Egyptology & Funerary Archaeology · University of Cairo

Dr. Saleh is one of the foremost authorities on ancient Egyptian mortuary practices and the mummification process. Over a 22-year academic career, she has participated in more than 40 active excavation seasons in the Valley of the Kings, Saqqara, and the Fayum region. She is the author of Wrapped in Eternity: The Science and Ritual of Egyptian Mummification and has advised museums across Europe and North America on the care and display of mummified remains. Dr. Saleh is passionate about making Egyptology accessible and exciting for young audiences, and travels internationally to share her expertise with the next generation of historians and archaeologists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Good to Know

What age group is this event designed for?
The core program is designed for children ages 6–14 with adult accompaniment. The Mummy Unwrapping Experience is best suited for ages 8 and up, as it involves a frank scientific discussion of human remains and ancient death practices. Parental discretion is advised for younger children in the premium session.
Is the mummy unwrapping a real mummy?
Yes. The demonstration features a genuine ancient Egyptian mummy loaned through a formal archaeological partnership. The unwrapping is conducted with full scientific and ethical protocols under the direct supervision of Dr. Saleh and a conservation team.
How do I know if a session still has Pharaoh's Circle spots?
The ticket page reflects real-time availability. Each session is capped at 30 premium spots. Once sold out, the premium option will be greyed out at checkout. We strongly recommend booking early.
Is food available at the venue?
A light refreshment cart serving themed snacks and beverages will operate in the lobby. Nut-free and gluten-free options are available. Outside food is permitted in lobby areas.
Are there group rates for school or scouting trips?
Groups of 10 or more receive 20% off General Admission. Contact events@riversideculturalcenter.org at least two weeks in advance to arrange group booking and receive an optional educational pre-visit packet aligned with school curriculum standards.
